Daily sudoku — three fresh puzzles every day

One killer, one classic, one Sudoku X — dealt at midnight UTC, the same three puzzles for everyone on Earth, every one verified solvable.

Today's three

  • Daily Killer — the main event. Difficulty climbs through the week: easy Mondays, expert Sundays.
  • Daily Classic — the traditional grid, easy to hard across the week.
  • Daily Sudoku X — the diagonal variant, matching the killer's weekly curve.

Solve any of the three and your streak survives; solve all three and you may brag freely. Missed days live on in the archive. Your streak, times and stats stay in your browser — no account required — with a backup code on the stats page for moving devices.

Why daily puzzles work

A daily puzzle is a shared, finite ritual: everyone in the world gets the same grid, it takes ten minutes, and tomorrow there's another. The weekday difficulty curve means Monday is a warm-up and Sunday is an event — and because every Zaney puzzle is machine-verified to have exactly one solution, the ritual never ends in a broken grid.
